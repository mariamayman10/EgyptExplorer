Popular Visited Places

Major tourist destinations include the millennia-old monuments in the Nile Valley. Principal among them are the Pyramids and Great Sphinx at Giza

Luxor, about 500 km south of Cairo, is the site of the ancient city of Thebes. It includes the ruins of the temple complexes at Karnak and Luxor, which stand within the modern city. On the opposite side of the Nile River lie the monuments, temples and tombs on the West Bank Necropolis, which include the Valley of the Kings and Valley of the Queens.

Sinai has the beach resorts of Sharm el-Sheikh, Dahab, Nuweiba and Taba as well as locations mentioned in the Bible such as Mount Sinai ("Jabal Musa"). Saint Catherine's Monastery may be the oldest working Christian monastery in the world.

Most Popular Tours

Giza

City that is famous for its iconic pyramids, including the Great Pyramid of Khufu, and the enigmatic Sphinx that stands guard nearby.

View Tours

Alexandria

City steeped in history, home to the Bibliotheca Alexandrina, Catacombs of Kom El Shoqafa, and a picturesque Mediterranean coastline.

View Tours

Aswan

Aswan, with its serene Nile views and the stunning Philae Temple, is a tranquil gem in Upper Egypt.

View Tours

Hurghada

Hurghada, situated on the Red Sea coast, is a vibrant resort town known for its underwater beauty, offering some of the best diving and snorkeling experiences.

View Tours

Siwa

Siwa, an oasis in the Western Desert, is famed for its unique culture and landscapes, including the Temple of the Oracle and the healing saltwater springs.

View Tours

Sharm EL-Sheikh

Sharm El-Sheikh is a paradise for beach lovers and divers, boasting clear waters and coral reefs that teem with marine life.

View Tours

New Capital

Egypt's new administrative capital, still under development, promises modernity and innovation, with state-of-the-art infrastructure and grand architectural designs.

Luxor

Luxor is often referred to as an open-air museum due to its remarkable archaeological sites, such as the Karnak and Luxor Temples, and the Valley of the Kings.
